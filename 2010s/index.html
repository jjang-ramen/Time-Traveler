A Handmade Web

An essay by J. R. Carpenter

The term handmade usually refers to objects made by hand or by using simple tools rather than machines.

I evoke the term 'handmade web' to refer to web pages coded by hand rather than by software; web pages made and maintained by individuals rather than by businesses or corporations.

Handmade web pages flourished in the mid-to-late-1990s, in the brief period after the academic web and before the corporate web.

bright, rich, personal, slow and under construction.

The handmade web emerged at a time when print and digital enjoyed a more symbiotic relationship.

Viewing old web pages today produces a temporal paradox. Layer upon layer of dated web-design aesthetics overlap and peel like wallpaper.

These are not artifacts of a dead web but signposts on a map of a living web.

J. R. Carpenter — March 2015
